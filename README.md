# Campaign Performance & Revenue Opportunity Analysis
This project analyzes two years of marketing campaign performance to evaluate how campaigns influenced revenue, ROI, engagement, and bookings. While the campaigns appeared highly successful overall, the analysis uncovered a major hidden execution flaw: 70% of ads lacked a Call-to-Action (CTA) button, creating a significant missed conversion and revenue opportunity.

Using Power BI, this project highlights both what worked well and what limited performance, turning campaign data into clear business insights.

🚀 Project Overview

The objective of this analysis was to:

- Measure campaign effectiveness
- Compare campaign performance using key KPIs
- Identify top-performing campaigns
- Detect conversion gaps affecting revenue
- Recommend ways to improve campaign efficiency

📈 Key Metrics
- Total Ad Spend: $356K
- Total Revenue: $7.8M
- ROI: 21X
- CTR: 17%
- CPC: $29

🔍 Key Findings
- Michelangelo was the strongest performer, delivering 43X ROI at the lowest cost.
- Leonardo stood out for engagement, achieving the highest CTR (29%) and becoming the second-highest revenue generator.
- Despite strong results, 70% of ads had no CTA button, meaning the business paid to attract users without consistently guiding them to book.
- Ads with CTAs showed approximately 80% CTR-to-booking conversion, revealing a major untapped revenue opportunity.

💡 Key Insight
- High ROI does not always mean maximum performance.
- The campaigns were successful, but the missing CTA issue showed that strong results can still hide costly execution gaps.

✅ Recommendation

- Make CTA buttons mandatory across all campaign ads to improve conversion efficiency and maximize revenue from existing ad spend.

🛠️ Tools Used

🔥 Final Takeaway

This project demonstrates how data can uncover the gap between good performance and full potential—showing that even high-performing campaigns can leave money on the table when execution details are overlooked.
